# Pre-Seed vs. Later Stage Decks
**Version:** 2026-03-19

---

## Overview

The pre-seed deck operates under fundamentally different constraints than later stages. At pre-seed, **investors are buying the team and the vision**, not a proven business.

---

## Stage Comparison Table

| Dimension | Pre-Seed | Seed | Series A |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary investor thesis | Team + vision | Early traction + insight | Repeatable growth |
| Financial projections | Skip or conceptual only | Basic model | Full P&L + unit economics |
| Traction expectation | LOIs, design partners, interviews | Revenue or strong pilots | Clear product-market fit signals |
| Team slide weight | Highest | High | Medium |
| "Why Now?" importance | Critical (substitutes for data) | Important | Less critical |
| Deck length | 10-12 slides | 12-15 slides | 15-20 slides |
| Competitive depth | Acknowledge + basic moat | Moderate | Full competitive analysis |

---

## Pre-Seed Specific Adjustments

### 1. Skip detailed financial projections
Forum Ventures explicitly says to skip them for the first meeting at pre-seed. Entrée Capital flags "fantasy financials" as a red flag when there is no revenue to anchor them.

Instead, demonstrate you understand unit economics conceptually:
- Your pricing logic
- Assumptions about cost to acquire a customer
- A credible path to revenue

### 2. Reframe traction expectations
At pre-seed, traction is not revenue. Acceptable proof includes:
- Letters of intent (LOIs)
- Design partnerships
- Customer discovery interviews with specific insights and named companies
- Working prototype or MVP
- Waitlist with conversion data

What does **not** count: zero evidence of customer contact.

### 3. Elevate the team slide
DocSend's data confirms VCs are spending dramatically more time evaluating founders at pre-seed. The team slide carries more weight than at any other stage.

If your team is your strongest asset, move it to slide 2 or 3.

### 4. Lead with "Why Now?"
At pre-seed, you often lack traction data that speaks for itself. The "Why Now?" slide substitutes narrative urgency for quantitative proof. It is the highest-leverage slide most founders can add.

---

## What Stays the Same Across Stages

- The need for a clear, visceral problem statement
- The requirement to acknowledge competition
- The importance of a specific go-to-market plan
- One primary business model, not multiple
- 10-15 slide length constraint
