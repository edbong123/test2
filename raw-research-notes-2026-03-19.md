## Raw Findings Dump

### Consensus slide list across sources
YC, Sequoia, a16z, First Round, 500 Global, Techstars all converge on: problem, solution, why now, market, product, business model, traction, GTM, competition, team, ask. Order varies. Lead with strongest signal.

### DocSend empirical data points
- Product slide: ~77 seconds avg
- Business model: +48% more time from VCs recently
- Traction on bad decks: +110% time vs good decks
- Team: +30-40% more VC time in 2024 at pre-seed/seed
- Market size: -19% time (VCs less patient with TAM slides)
- Full emailed deck: 2-4 minutes before meeting decision

### "Why Now?" gap
Focused Chaos 50-deck review: 75% of decks omit or underdeliver this slide. NFX says timing is one of six critical signals. First Round: (1) previously too expensive, (2) enabling tech now exists, (3) problem now more acute.

### GTM failure rate
~40% of decks phone in the GTM slide. Jerry Chen (Greylock): "obsess about distribution as much as product." Most common failure: buzzword list with no specifics.

### Competition red flag
Every source. Never say no competitors. Every problem has incumbents — even spreadsheets and email.

### Team timing
DocSend 2024: 30-40% more time on team at pre-seed. Ada Ventures + Crunchbase: don't bury it. Reid Hoffman: lead with strongest element.

### Two-deck rule
Techstars: "read me" version for email (stands alone), "listen to me" for live (minimal words, backdrop only).

### Projection warning
Forum Ventures: skip detailed financials for pre-seed first meeting. Entrée Capital: "fantasy financials" is a red flag.

### Vanity metric warning
DocSend, 500 Global: downloads, page views, social followers without context are noise. Gold standard at pre-seed B2B: signed LOIs or paid pilots with named customers.

### Design rules
Kevin Hale (YC): legible, simple, obvious. Entrée Capital: max 30 words per slide. Forum Ventures: budget $100-300 for freelance designer.

### Narrative frameworks
Ben Yoskovitz: Hearts → Minds → Wallets. Slidebean: 3-act screenplay. Oren Klaff (Pitch Anything): reptilian brain processes first. BenchSci CEO: don't use section-header titles, use story-advancing headlines.

### Ask slide rules
Techstars: state dollar figure not round name. Never pitch valuation before term sheet. Use of funds in 3-4 categories. First Round: end with discussion starter.

### Pre-seed vs seed vs A
Pre-seed: team + vision. No detailed projections. Traction = LOIs + interviews + prototype. Why Now replaces data.
Seed: early metrics, initial PMF signals, basic model.
Series A: data-heavy, retention curves, cohort analysis, repeatable sales motion.
